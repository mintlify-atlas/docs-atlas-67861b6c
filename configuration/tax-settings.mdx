Tax settings control how sales tax is calculated and displayed on receipts. Configure your business information, primary and secondary tax rates, and tax exemption options.

## Accessing tax settings

Navigate to **Manager Dashboard** â†’ **Tax Settings** to configure these options. Manager role is required.

<Note>
  Tax settings are displayed on receipts and used for all sales calculations. Ensure accuracy for compliance with local tax regulations.
</Note>

## Business information

Configure your business details that appear on receipts and reports.

<ParamField path="businessName" type="string" required>
  Official business name printed on receipts
  
  Example: `BMS Pet Store`
</ParamField>

<ParamField path="taxNumber" type="string" optional>
  Tax registration or identification number
  
  Example: `123-456-7890` or `VAT-GB123456789`
  
  Required in many jurisdictions for tax compliance.
</ParamField>

<ParamField path="businessAddress" type="string" optional>
  Complete business address for receipts
  
  Example: `123 Main Street, Suite 100, City, State 12345`
</ParamField>

## Tax configuration

Control whether tax is charged on sales and configure tax rates.

<ParamField path="enableTax" type="boolean" default="true">
  Master toggle for tax calculations
  
  When disabled, no tax is calculated or displayed on any transactions.
</ParamField>

<Warning>
  Disabling tax does not exempt your business from tax reporting obligations. Consult with a tax professional before disabling tax calculations.
</Warning>

### Primary tax

<ParamField path="taxName" type="string" default="Sales Tax">
  Display name for the primary tax
  
  Common examples:
  - `Sales Tax` (United States)
  - `VAT` (Europe, UK)
  - `GST` (Canada, Australia, India)
  - `Tax` (Generic)
</ParamField>

<ParamField path="taxRate" type="decimal" default="10">
  Primary tax rate as a percentage
  
  **Range:** 0-100%
  
  Example: `10` for 10% tax, `12.5` for 12.5% tax
</ParamField>

### Secondary tax (optional)

Some regions require multiple tax calculations, such as state and federal tax, or VAT plus service tax.

<ParamField path="enableSecondaryTax" type="boolean" default="false">
  Enable a second tax calculation
  
  When enabled, both primary and secondary taxes are calculated and displayed separately on receipts.
</ParamField>

<ParamField path="secondaryTaxName" type="string" default="Service Tax">
  Display name for the secondary tax
  
  Common examples:
  - `Service Tax`
  - `City Tax`
  - `Federal Tax`
  - `Local Tax`
</ParamField>

<ParamField path="secondaryTaxRate" type="decimal" default="5">
  Secondary tax rate as a percentage
  
  **Range:** 0-100%
</ParamField>

<Note>
  Both primary and secondary taxes are calculated on the subtotal and displayed as separate line items on receipts.
</Note>

## Tax exemptions

Allow tax-exempt sales for qualified customers or organizations.

<ParamField path="enableTaxExemptions" type="boolean" default="false">
  Enable "Tax Exempt" option in POS
  
  When enabled, cashiers can mark transactions as tax-exempt. This is commonly used for:
  - Non-profit organizations
  - Government purchases
  - Resale certificates
  - Other qualified exemptions
</ParamField>

<Warning>
  Tax-exempt transactions should only be processed with proper documentation. Maintain records of tax exemption certificates for compliance.
</Warning>

## Additional settings

<ParamField path="notes" type="string" optional>
  Internal notes about tax configuration
  
  Use this field to document:
  - Tax compliance requirements
  - Special exemption rules
  - Regional tax regulations
  - Date of last tax rate update
</ParamField>

## Tax calculation examples

### Single tax (10% sales tax)

```
Subtotal:    $100.00
Sales Tax:   $ 10.00 (10%)
Total:       $110.00
```

### Multiple taxes (10% VAT + 5% service tax)

```
Subtotal:      $100.00
VAT:           $ 10.00 (10%)
Service Tax:   $  5.00 (5%)
Total:         $115.00
```

### Tax exempt

```
Subtotal:    $100.00
Tax:         $  0.00 (Exempt)
Total:       $100.00
```

## API endpoint

```http
GET /api/settings/tax
POST /api/tax-settings
```

**Example request:**

```json
{
  "businessName": "BMS Pet Store",
  "taxNumber": "123-456-7890",
  "businessAddress": "123 Main Street, City, State 12345",
  "enableTax": true,
  "taxName": "Sales Tax",
  "taxRate": 10.0,
  "enableSecondaryTax": false,
  "secondaryTaxName": "Service Tax",
  "secondaryTaxRate": 5.0,
  "enableTaxExemptions": false,
  "notes": "Standard state sales tax rate. Last updated: Jan 2024"
}
```

## Current tax configuration summary

The tax settings page displays a summary of your current configuration:

- **Tax Status:** Enabled or Disabled
- **Primary Tax:** Name and rate
- **Secondary Tax:** Name and rate (if enabled)
- **Tax Exemptions:** Allowed or Not Allowed
